Intranet · CMS · Content Strategy 2017 – 2024

OSU Moritz College of Law Intranet

The Ohio State University, Moritz College of Law

Role Web Management Specialist
Scope Strategy · Build · Content · Ongoing management
Tools WordPress · CMS · Google Analytics
Context

Moritz is ranked #34 nationally by US News, with 562 full-time JD students and 93 faculty members. The college was under pressure to improve its public profile and resource accessibility, while competing for students and faculty against more visible peer institutions.

The existing intranet was legacy and hard-coded, making updates slow, inconsistent, and entirely dependent on technical staff. Faculty and students struggled to find basic resources, and the college had no scalable way to maintain brand consistency across communications.

The Brief

Migrate the intranet to a CMS, redesign the information architecture to serve two distinct audiences (students and staff/faculty), and build a self-serve communications toolkit so departments could produce on-brand materials without relying on the central team.

What Was Built

Intranet Homepage

Designed around two distinct user paths, Students and Staff & Faculty, so visitors could orient immediately. Quick-link icon cards surfaced the most common requests without requiring search. Community photo strips and a search bar provided fallback navigation.

Resources for Students

Consolidated curriculum planning, department directories, and common requests into a single, scannable page. Department cards with photos and sub-links reduced the number of clicks to reach key resources. Icon-based request tiles covered the most repeated student actions.

Staff & Faculty Resources

A dedicated section for administrative and academic staff covering planning resources, faculty grant applications, HR, IT, health and wellness, and committee access, all surfaced through a consistent icon-card system with a searchable directory below.

Communications & Marketing Toolkit

Built a self-serve branded asset hub for the college, providing downloadable print templates, digital templates, department-specific images, and logo files. Any department could produce on-brand communications without submitting a request to the central team, reducing bottlenecks and improving consistency across all owned channels.

My Contribution
  • Led the full migration from a legacy hard-coded site to a CMS, including scoping, stakeholder alignment, and build
  • Designed the information architecture: audience segmentation, navigation hierarchy, and content taxonomy
  • Built and maintained all page templates, ensuring accessibility compliance throughout
  • Managed distribution lists, access permissions, and communication requests across all owned channels
  • Created the Communications and Marketing Toolkit to reduce dependency on the central team and standardise branded asset production
  • Coordinated ongoing content updates, production schedules, and cross-departmental requests across a 7-year tenure
Result

Delivered measurable improvements in UX and resource findability within the first quarter post-launch. The CMS migration gave the college full editorial independence: content updates that previously required developer intervention could be handled directly by the communications team. The toolkit reduced ad hoc template requests and standardised how the college presented itself across all channels.

Intranet · UX Research · Content Strategy 2017 – 2018

Enterprise Intranet Redesign

Nationwide Insurance

Role Intranet Strategy Intern
Scope User research · IA · Content planning · Figma
Tools Figma · Surveys · User Interviews
Context

Nationwide Insurance's enterprise intranet was structured around internal org charts rather than how employees actually worked. Resources were buried, navigation was inconsistent across departments, and the communications team had no centralised way to plan or coordinate content across channels.

Employees were raising repeat support requests for information that should have been findable in under two clicks. The redesign needed to address both the underlying information architecture and the absence of a content planning process.

The Brief

Run user research to understand how employees navigated the existing intranet, translate findings into a new information architecture, and design a content calendar in Figma to give the communications team a repeatable planning process going forward.

What Was Built

Proposed Information Architecture

Derived from 12 employee interviews and an 84-response survey, the proposed IA restructured the intranet around two primary user paths — For Employees and For Managers — with a shared News & Updates hub and a persistent Quick Links panel for the most common requests. The structure reduced average depth-to-resource from four clicks to two.

Editorial Content Calendar

Designed in Figma, the editorial calendar gave the communications team a shared monthly planning view across all owned channels: email newsletter, intranet posts, manager briefings, town halls, surveys, and policy updates. Content blocks are colour-coded by type and can span multiple weeks. The format was built to be updated each month without design support.

My Contribution
  • Designed and facilitated 12 one-on-one user interviews with employees across departments to map navigation pain points
  • Analysed an 84-response survey to quantify the most common resource-finding failures
  • Produced the proposed IA, presenting recommendations to communications and IT stakeholders
  • Designed the editorial content calendar in Figma, covering six channel types with a colour-coded weekly planning grid
  • Coordinated with IT to ensure the proposed IA was implementable within the existing CMS structure
Result

The proposed IA was adopted by the team and handed to IT for implementation. The editorial calendar was taken into active use by the communications team, replacing an ad hoc email chain process. Resource discoverability improved measurably in follow-up surveys conducted post-launch.

Analytics · Reporting · Excel 2024

Internal Communications Channel Report

Meridian Insurance Group (Sample Project)

Role Internal Comms Analyst
Scope Data modelling · Analysis · Reporting · Visualisation
Tools Excel · VLOOKUP · Pivot Charts
Context

Internal communications teams are often producing content without a clear view of what is performing and what is not. Without a structured reporting process, it is difficult to justify resource allocation, demonstrate impact to leadership, or identify which channels and content types deserve more investment.

This sample project simulates a full-year performance review for a mid-size insurance company's internal comms function, covering three channels: an email newsletter, an intranet, and LinkedIn.

The Brief

Build a reusable, stakeholder-ready reporting workbook that tracks monthly channel performance against industry benchmarks, surfaces trends over time, and can be handed to a non-technical team for ongoing updates.

What Was Built

Dashboard & Channel Comparison

A side-by-side view of average engagement rate vs industry benchmark across all three channels, alongside a monthly trend line for the email newsletter showing where performance dipped below benchmark (summer) and recovered strongly in Q4. Green shading indicates above-benchmark periods; red indicates below.

Raw Data Sheet with VLOOKUP

36 rows of monthly campaign data across all channels. The Benchmark Eng. Rate column uses a VLOOKUP to pull the correct industry benchmark from a separate reference table based on the channel in column A. The vs Benchmark column calculates the delta, with conditional formatting applied to highlight over- and under-performance at a glance.

My Contribution
  • Designed a unified data model to track three channels with different engagement metrics in a single workbook
  • Built a VLOOKUP reference table mapping each channel to its industry benchmark, used across Raw Data and Channel Summary sheets
  • Created pivot-style channel summary with aggregated totals and average rates by channel
  • Applied conditional formatting to surface above/below benchmark performance without manual review
  • Produced a stakeholder-ready dashboard tab with KPI callouts and two chart types (bar and line)
  • Structured the workbook for handover: clearly labelled sheets, formula-driven cells, no manual overrides
Result

A fully reusable reporting template that any communications team could update monthly with minimal effort. Key findings from the simulated data: email newsletter averaged 51.2% open rate against a 47% benchmark, with a predictable summer dip and strong Q4 recovery. LinkedIn engagement averaged 3.8% against a 3.5% benchmark. The workbook structure makes it straightforward to extend to additional channels or time periods.
