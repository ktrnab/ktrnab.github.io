Intranet · Public Website · CMS · Content Strategy 2017 – 2024

Ohio State Website Migration

The Ohio State University, Moritz College of Law

Role Web Management Specialist
Scope Strategy · CMS build · Content · Accessibility · Ongoing management
Tools WordPress · CMS · Google Analytics
The Challenge

An outdated, hard-coded web platform was creating friction for users and hindering conversion across both the public-facing college website and the internal staff and student intranet. Content updates were slow, inconsistent, and bottlenecked by a total reliance on technical staff — with zero tolerance for downtime or content loss during the transition.

Ranked #34 nationally, Moritz College of Law needed platforms that matched its standing: a public website that competed for prospective students, and an intranet that actually served the people who used it daily.

The Brief

Platform Migration: Transition both the public site and the legacy intranet to a flexible, maintainable CMS.

Information Architecture: Redesign the intranet structure to serve two distinct user groups (Students vs. Staff/Faculty) and rebuild the public site around conversion and discoverability.

Brand Governance: Build a self-serve communications toolkit to empower individual departments to produce on-brand materials without relying on the central design team.

What Was Built

1. Public-Facing Website Redesign

The college's public website was rebuilt from a cluttered, hard-coded page into a clean, conversion-optimised CMS site. The new design introduced a clear visual hierarchy, prominent calls to action for prospective students, and a modern layout aligned with the university's brand standards — replacing the previous static, update-resistant structure.

2. Audience-Segmented Intranet

The intranet homepage was designed around two immediate, distinct user paths so visitors could self-select their journey right away.

For Students: A consolidated, highly scannable dashboard — curriculum planning, directories, and common request forms converted into visual icon-cards, drastically reducing click-depth for high-frequency actions.

For Staff & Faculty: A dedicated administrative hub covering HR, IT, grant applications, and committee access, backed by a persistent, searchable directory.

3. Self-Serve Design Toolkit

To solve the communication bottleneck, I built a centralised, branded asset hub. This provided every department with downloadable print and digital templates, curated image libraries, and logo files — allowing non-designers to produce standardised, on-brand materials independently, eliminating the central team's backlog.

My Contribution
7-Year Tenure
  • Full project lifecycle: Managed end-to-end delivery — aligning cross-functional requirements, coordinating stakeholders, overseeing the CMS build, and ensuring zero content loss during migration
  • UX & IA strategy: Defined the audience segmentation, navigation hierarchy, and underlying content taxonomy for both the public site and intranet
  • Accessibility compliance: Built and maintained all CMS page templates to strict accessibility standards throughout the project and post-launch
  • Operational management: Coordinated ongoing content updates, production schedules, and cross-departmental requests over seven years, alongside managing access permissions and distribution lists
Result

Delivered on time with zero downtime and no content loss. The new public-facing site drove measurable UX improvements and conversion rate gains within the first quarter post-launch, with prospective student enquiries increasing as a direct result of the redesigned journey. The CMS migration granted the college full editorial independence, and the self-serve toolkit virtually eliminated ad hoc template requests — ensuring a unified brand presence across all college channels.

Intranet · UX Research · Content Strategy 2017 – 2018

Enterprise Intranet Redesign

Nationwide Insurance

Role Intranet Strategy Intern
Scope User research · IA · Content planning · Figma
Tools Miro · Surveys · User Interviews
The Challenge

The Nationwide enterprise intranet historically followed an organisation-centric model, where navigation was mapped to internal business units. While this reflected the company's structure, it created findability hurdles for employees whose daily tasks often crossed departmental lines.

With resources distributed across disconnected SharePoint sites and no centralised planning process, information was often buried — leading to a steady increase in avoidable support tickets.

The Brief

Research: Map employee mental models to identify where the org-chart logic failed daily workflows.

Information Architecture: Evolve the system into a task-based IA that prioritises the most frequent employee actions.

Systems Design: Develop a sustainable, Figma-based editorial calendar to synchronise communications across six internal channels.

What Was Built

1. Task-Based Information Architecture

The new IA was mapped in Miro and moved away from departmental silos in favour of user intent. Based on interviews and survey data, I restructured the site into two distinct high-level paths: "For Employees" — a high-speed lane for frequent tasks like payroll, benefits, and HR forms — and "For Managers" — a dedicated space for cross-functional planning and team resources. A persistent Quick Links panel surfaced top-tier requests, reducing the path to key resources from four clicks to two.

2. Cross-Channel Editorial System

To solve the scattered content problem, I designed an operational calendar in Miro. This replaced an ad hoc email process with a shared source of truth for the communications team, featuring channel synchronisation across newsletters, town halls, and intranet posts, and a modular template the team could update monthly without requiring design software expertise.

My Contribution
  • Discovery: Conducted 12 deep-dive interviews and an 84-person survey to quantify navigation friction and map employee mental models
  • Stakeholder management: Presented research-backed IA recommendations to IT and Comms leads to secure implementation buy-in
  • Design systems: Built the Miro editorial framework with colour-coded content categories and accessibility-compliant (WCAG 2.1) visual cues
  • Technical validation: Partnered with IT to ensure the new structure was compatible with existing CMS constraints
Result

The task-based IA was adopted as the new enterprise standard. By aligning the intranet with actual employee workflows, we addressed the root cause of the findability gap — not just the symptoms. The editorial calendar is now in active monthly use, and follow-up data shows a significant improvement in resource discoverability across the organisation.

Analytics · Reporting · Excel 2024

Internal Communications Channel Report

Meridian Insurance Group (Sample Project)

Role Internal Comms Analyst
Scope Data modelling · Analysis · Reporting · Visualisation
Tools Excel · VLOOKUP · Pivot Charts
Context

Internal communications teams are often producing content without a clear view of what is performing and what is not. Without a structured reporting process, it is difficult to justify resource allocation, demonstrate impact to leadership, or identify which channels and content types deserve more investment.

This sample project simulates a full-year performance review for a mid-size insurance company's internal comms function, covering three channels: an email newsletter, an intranet, and LinkedIn.

The Brief

Build a reusable, stakeholder-ready reporting workbook that tracks monthly channel performance against industry benchmarks, surfaces trends over time, and can be handed to a non-technical team for ongoing updates.

What Was Built

Dashboard & Channel Comparison

A side-by-side view of average engagement rate vs industry benchmark across all three channels, alongside a monthly trend line for the email newsletter showing where performance dipped below benchmark (summer) and recovered strongly in Q4. Green shading indicates above-benchmark periods; red indicates below.

Raw Data Sheet with VLOOKUP

36 rows of monthly campaign data across all channels. The Benchmark Eng. Rate column uses a VLOOKUP to pull the correct industry benchmark from a separate reference table based on the channel in column A. The vs Benchmark column calculates the delta, with conditional formatting applied to highlight over- and under-performance at a glance.

My Contribution
  • Designed a unified data model to track three channels with different engagement metrics in a single workbook
  • Built a VLOOKUP reference table mapping each channel to its industry benchmark, used across Raw Data and Channel Summary sheets
  • Created pivot-style channel summary with aggregated totals and average rates by channel
  • Applied conditional formatting to surface above/below benchmark performance without manual review
  • Produced a stakeholder-ready dashboard tab with KPI callouts and two chart types (bar and line)
  • Structured the workbook for handover: clearly labelled sheets, formula-driven cells, no manual overrides
Result

A fully reusable reporting template that any communications team could update monthly with minimal effort. Key findings from the simulated data: email newsletter averaged 51.2% open rate against a 47% benchmark, with a predictable summer dip and strong Q4 recovery. LinkedIn engagement averaged 3.8% against a 3.5% benchmark. The workbook structure makes it straightforward to extend to additional channels or time periods.
